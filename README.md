The genomic islands are ecologically non-neutral: metabolomic analysis, phage susceptibility assays, and competition experiments confirm micro-niche differentiation and viral predation resistance as selective forces maintaining HVR diversity.

> — Viver, T. et al., *Nature Communications* **15**, 483, 2024

The filaments glide vertically within communal polysaccharide sheaths: ascending to the sediment-water interface to accumulate $\text{NO}_3^-$ from bottom water, then descending $5$–$15$ cm into the anoxic sediment to oxidize $\text{H}_2\text{S}$, coupling the nitrogen and sulfur biogeochemical cycles.

## Module A — Mathematical Background

**A1. The pan-genome and core/accessory partition.** For a species with $n$ strains, the pan-genome is $\mathcal{G} = \bigcup_i G_i$ (all genes across all strains). The core genome is $\mathcal{C} = \bigcap_i G_i$ (genes shared by all strains); the accessory genome is $\mathcal{A} = \mathcal{G} \setminus \mathcal{C}$ (strain-specific genes). A pan-genome is "open" if $|\mathcal{G}|$ grows without bound as $n \to \infty$; "closed" if it saturates. *Salinibacter ruber* has an open pan-genome: each new strain adds new accessory genes. The core-to-accessory ratio $|\mathcal{C}|/|\mathcal{A}|$ IS the kernel-to-petal ratio $|K|/|P_i|$ in ERI: a fixed, stable shared foundation (core) with unlimited flexible specialization (accessory).

**A2. Homologous recombination as Fisher column-space stabilization.** In the *S. ruber* population, HR acts preferentially in the core genome: alleles within the core are shuffled between strains at a rate $2\times$ higher than the point mutation rate (Viver et al. 2024). HR in the core IS PRIMA Fisher column-space stabilization: it averages out individual-strain Fisher perturbations across the population, maintaining the core's Fisher eigenspace at its population-average configuration. The accessory genome experiences HGT rather than HR — importing new Fisher eigenvectors from outside the species' gene pool — exactly as the bdelloid rotifer's HGT imports Fisher eigenvectors from $F_{\text{env}}$ (CRYPTA).

**A3. The nitrate vacuole as a high-capacity thermodynamic battery.** Thioploca concentrates $\text{NO}_3^-$ from $\sim 25\,\mu\text{M}$ ambient to $500\,\text{mM}$ intracellular — a concentration factor of $20{,}000\times$, requiring work $W = RT\ln(c_{\text{in}}/c_{\text{out}}) = 8.314 \times 298 \times \ln(20{,}000) \approx 24.7\,\text{kJ/mol}$ of stored free energy per mole of $\text{NO}_3^-$ loaded. The vacuole occupying $> 80\%$ of cell volume IS the CHORD Q16.16 register file: a large, dedicated storage substrate holding the primary information carrier ($\text{NO}_3^-$ / the Fisher outer-product buffer $F_t$) at concentrations far above ambient, ready for deployment when the processing environment ($\text{H}_2\text{S}$ / the gradient $\nabla L$) is encountered.

**A4. Magnetosome chain as a passive eigenvalue probe.** The magnetosome chain of $\sim 20$ aligned $\text{Fe}_3\text{O}_4$ crystals, each $\sim 45$ nm, produces a magnetic dipole moment $m \approx 10^{-15}$ A·m² in *Magnetospirillum*. The thermal energy at $298\,\text{K}$ is $k_BT \approx 4.1 \times 10^{-21}$ J. The ratio $mB/k_BT \gg 1$ for Earth's magnetic field $B \approx 50\,\mu\text{T}$: $mB = 5 \times 10^{-20}$ J, giving $mB/k_BT \approx 10$–$12$. This ratio IS the Fisher signal-to-noise ratio for the dominant eigenvector: the geomagnetic field direction is the single Fisher eigenvector with eigenvalue $> \varepsilon$ in the magnetotactic organism's orientation parameter space, and $mB/k_BT \gg 1$ guarantees that this direction is above the singularity barrier $\varepsilon = 2^{-16}$ — the magnetosome chain is the physical implementation of a single-eigenvalue PRIMA system.

**A5. Genome streamlining as minimum sufficient Fisher rank.** SAR11's genome ($\sim 1.3$ Mb, $\sim 1{,}350$ genes) is the minimum genome compatible with free-living chemoheterotrophic metabolism in oligotrophic ocean water. The information content of the genome IS rank$(F)$ — the number of Fisher eigenvectors that the organism's parameter space actively maintains. SAR11's minimum sufficient genome IS the minimum sufficient Fisher rank: the smallest rank$(F)$ compatible with free-living survival in its ecological niche. Every deleted gene is a Fisher eigenvector deleted from the column space because it carries redundant information already covered by overlapping genes (pleiotropy IS Fisher eigenvector sharing between genes).
